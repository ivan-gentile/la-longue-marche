# Bourbaki Schémas transcription — page gap census

Source: `raw_pdf/bourbaki_schemes.pdf` (437 scanned pages, no text layer).
Deliverable: `tex_output/bourbaki_schemes_full_flash-lite.tex`.
Generated by `experiments/bourbaki/analyze_gaps.py`.

## Two defects, both explained by whole-document mode

1. **Page markers are not PDF page numbers.** The 367
   markers in the tex were produced by the model reading the page
   numbers printed on the typescript itself — numbering that
   restarts per chapter and includes compound pages
   ("11-106 bis"): the numeric markers hold only
   137 unique values (max 142)
   with 5 mid-file resets. The file therefore cannot be
   navigated against the scans — this is why gaps feel
   "unusual": text jumps without any traceable position.
2. **Roughly 70 of 437 pages
   were silently merged or skipped.** The chunks emitted
   367 page markers for 437 assigned pages,
   the losses concentrated in two chunks (below); whole-document
   mode summarises past pages without raising an error. Per chunk:

| Chunk | PDF pages | Assigned | Pages marked | Typescript numbers seen |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| chunk_000_p1-50 | 1-50 | 50 | 48 | 1 … 50 |
| chunk_001_p51-100 | 51-100 | 50 | 22 | 31 … 52 |
| chunk_002_p101-150 | 101-150 | 50 | 48 | 1 … 50 |
| chunk_003_p151-200 | 151-200 | 50 | 28 | 115 … 142 |
| chunk_004_p201-250 | 201-250 | 50 | 45 | 12 … 51 |
| chunk_005_p251-300 | 251-300 | 50 | 49 | 62 … 105 quater |
| chunk_006_p301-350 | 301-350 | 50 | 46 | 11-105 quinquiès … III-22 |
| chunk_007_p351-400 | 351-400 | 50 | 44 | 23 … 66 |
| chunk_008_p401-437 | 401-437 | 37 | 37 | 73 … 109 |

## Recommended fix

Re-run the whole document **page-by-page** (the La Longue Marche
production mode: one API call per PDF page, marker written by the
pipeline, not the model). That mode cannot skip a page silently and
gives every page a verifiable PDF index. Estimated cost on
Flash-Lite at recorded rates: well under $1 for all 437 pages.
Until then, `bourbaki_schemes_full_flash-lite.tex` should be treated
as a continuous-text draft without page alignment, not as a
page-faithful transcription.
